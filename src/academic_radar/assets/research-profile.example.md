# Research interest profile

## Core problem

How to integrate decision makers' contextual, heterogeneous, and dynamically evolving preferences into optimization search through human-machine collaboration, so solutions retain mathematical quality and operational acceptability.

## High-priority themes

1. Preference elicitation, representation, learning, integration, revision, conflict detection, and preference lifecycle management in optimization or decision support.
2. Interactive and human-in-the-loop optimization, especially vehicle routing, combinatorial optimization, multi-objective decision making, and operationally acceptable solutions.
3. Human-AI collaboration and human-machine systems: agency, control, trust calibration, explainability, cognitive workload, decision quality, and user experiments.
4. Natural-language or multimodal expression of constraints and preferences: semantic parsing, grounding/deixis, compound intent decomposition, uncertainty, abstention, clarification, and back-translation.
5. Mixed-initiative and proactive systems: intervention timing, clarification, negotiation, recommendations, controllability, interruption, and levels of automation.
6. Route attractiveness, visual or operational qualities of routes, municipal sanitation, logistics, arc routing, and real-world vehicle-routing practice.

## Methodological interests

LLMs as semantic parsers or interaction components; controlled HCI experiments; behavioral decision theory; interactive optimization taxonomies; information projection and constrained probabilistic modeling; optimization-derived signals such as dual values, conflict certificates, and confidence.

## Relevance boundaries

- Prioritize conceptual or methodological transfer, not keyword overlap alone.
- General HCI papers are relevant only when they inform decision support, preference-centered interaction, human-AI collaboration, language grounding, or proactive intervention.
- Pure algorithmic optimization papers are relevant only when they address preferences, interactive/human-in-the-loop processes, explainability, operational acceptability, or the named routing settings.
- Pure recommender-system preference prediction and generic LLM benchmark papers are usually low priority unless they contribute transferable elicitation, uncertainty, grounding, clarification, or evaluation methods.
